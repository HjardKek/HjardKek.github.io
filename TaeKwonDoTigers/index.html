WHAT IS TAEKWONDO?

Taekwondo is a Korean martial art form that translates to "The Way of the Foot and Fist". It primarily focuses on striking techniques such as punching and kicking, with an emphasis on speed, flexibility, and control. It is one of the most popular martial art forms in the world - with over 70 million practitioners worldwide - and has been an olympic sport since 2000. Although the modern form of Taekwondo was not founded until 1955, the roots of Taekwondo can be found in various Korean martial art forms stretching back over 2000 years.

WHY STUDY TAEKWONDO?

There are many benefits to studying Taekwondo, whether you simply want to stay fit, become more flexible, or learn to defend yourself. Some other benefits include:

  • Improved strength and blance
  • Improved stamina
  • Improved confidence and self-esteem
  • Improved agility and reflexes
  • Improved concentration and focus
  • Greater self-discipline
  • Reduced stress
